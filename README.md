## 1. Executive Summary
This report summarizes an e-commerce performance analysis conducted on a synthetic dataset from 2023 to early 2026. The analysis focused on revenue trends, customer behavior, churn patterns, product performance, and geographic sales distribution. Overall, the business shows recovery in 2025 with improved customer retention, but faces risks from declining average order value and high revenue dependence on a small group of customers.
## 2. Project Objectives
  - Evaluate whether the business is profitable and growing
  - Analyze customer buying behavior and activity trends
  - Assess customer value and churn patterns
  - Identify top and low-performing products
  - Determine regional revenue distribution
## 3. Data and Tools
The dataset was synthetically generated using AI and analyzed using Microsoft Excel, Power Query, Power Pivot, and DAX. Data cleaning, transformation, and modeling were performed, and a calendar table was created for time-series analysis.
## 4. Key Findings
### 4.1 Revenue and Growth
Revenue declined by 2.96% in 2024 but recovered with a 5.97% growth in 2025. Average Order Value (AOV) dropped in 2024 and partially recovered in 2025 but remains below 2023 levels, indicating increased price sensitivity and smaller basket sizes.
### 4.2 Order Activity
Order volume increased slightly each year, but revenue declined in 2024 despite higher orders. This indicates basket value dilution likely caused by discounting or lower-margin products.
### 4.3 Customer Value and Loyalty
The business has 2,630 unique customers, with 52.9% being repeat buyers, indicating strong loyalty. However, the top 10 customers contribute 31.75% of total revenue, creating high customer concentration risk.
### 4.4 Customer Churn
Churn declined from 35.3% in 2023 to 19.67% in 2025, suggesting improved customer retention and increasing lifetime value.
### 4.5 Product Performance
Laptops and Smart TVs generate 53.8% of revenue and should be prioritized in inventory. Low-performing products such as dumbbells, organic snacks, yoga mats, and building blocks contribute less than 1% each and should be reviewed.
### 4.6 Geographic Revenue Distribution
Revenue is evenly distributed across regions, with Europe, Asia, South America, North America, and Africa each contributing approximately 19–21%.
### 4.7 RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary)
According to the RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) customer segmentation data for the whole period, the positive segments (Champions + Loyal) with 49% of total, reflects a strong loyalty base, but also a concerning churn risk (35% at risk and 16% lost).
## 5. Recommendations
  - Introduce tiered free shipping thresholds to increase average order value
  - Bundle complementary products to encourage larger baskets
  - Ensure continuous availability of top-performing products
  - Develop VIP programs for long-term loyal customers
  - Automate retention and personalized email campaigns
  - Conduct churn cohort and win-back campaigns to further reduce customer loss
  - Incorporate more technical functions and conditions based on the revolving business requirements
## 6. Conclusion
The business demonstrates recovery and improved customer loyalty but must increase basket value and reduce dependency on a small group of customers. Strategic pricing, retention, and inventory management will support sustainable long-term growth.
